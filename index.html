CONNECTING COMO

CoMO Connect is the public transit system operated by the City of Columbia. Two major connectors that serve the areas with the most passengers, while the other nine routes cover as many of the city neighborhoods as possible. CoMO Connect was launched in 2014 as a result of the city’s effort to rebrand the system and to conduct major changes to the serving areas. But just three years laterm the city is planning an overhaul due to significant decreases in ridership.

By HAOTIAN MAI/Missourian | May 8, 2017

Fewer rides the buses

Compare to 2014 data , COMO Connect saw a 16.5% decrease of total ridership in 2015 and a 28.2% decrease in 2016.

Passengers down, but budgets aren't

Despite the decline in ridership, the money the city allocates for the buses remains about the same.

Routes Disparity

How are individual routes doing in terms of ridership and punctuality?

The 11 routes of CoMO Connect show huge disparity in the bus service. Passengers favor certain routes while in some routes, the bus is nearly empty. Buses are 69 percent on-time. Delays vary from route to route.



Just two routes— the Black and Gold routes — comprise 70 percent of the passengers. The other nine routes each carry 1 percent to 6 percent of the bus system's total passengers. Buses running through the neighborhood are almost empty.

Looking Ahead

City’s proposed changes

City hired consultant company to design alternative options of the bus system. The city plans to use at least a full year to hear the public input.
